The Best Photo Spots Along Huangshan’s Cable Car Routes

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Huangshan, or the Yellow Mountains, is one of China’s most iconic destinations, renowned for its dramatic granite peaks, ancient pine trees, and ever-changing sea of clouds. While hiking is a popular way to explore, the cable cars offer a breathtaking (and efficient) alternative, whisking visitors to some of the most photogenic spots with minimal effort. Whether you're an avid photographer or just looking to capture unforgettable memories, here’s a guide to the best photo spots along Huangshan’s cable car routes.

The Yungu Cable Car: A Scenic Ascent

The Yungu Cable Car is one of the most popular routes, taking visitors from the eastern base of Huangshan up to the White Goose Ridge in just eight minutes. Along the way, you’ll pass towering cliffs, lush forests, and sweeping vistas—perfect for snapping stunning shots.

1. The Sea of Clouds from the Cable Car

One of the most magical moments is when the cable car glides above a rolling sea of clouds. This phenomenon is most common in early morning or after rain, creating an ethereal landscape that looks straight out of a painting. Keep your camera ready—the view changes every few seconds!

2. Pine Trees Clinging to the Cliffs

Huangshan’s famous pine trees, like the Welcoming Guest Pine, are legendary, but from the Yungu Cable Car, you’ll see lesser-known (yet equally striking) pines gripping the cliffs. The contrast between the rugged rocks and the resilient trees makes for a powerful composition.

3. Sunrise at White Goose Ridge

If you take the first cable car up at dawn, you’ll arrive just in time for sunrise at White Goose Ridge. The golden light spilling over the peaks is a photographer’s dream. Pro tip: Use a polarizing filter to enhance the colors.

The Taiping Cable Car: A Journey Through the North

Less crowded than the Yungu route, the Taiping Cable Car offers a different perspective, taking visitors from the northern foothills up to Songgu Peak. This route is ideal for those who love deep valleys and rugged landscapes.

1. The Grand Canyon Views

As the cable car ascends, you’ll get a bird’s-eye view of the Xihai Grand Canyon, a labyrinth of steep cliffs and winding paths. The sheer scale of the canyon is awe-inspiring—wide-angle lenses work best here.

2. The "Flying Over Rock" Illusion

One unique shot along this route is the illusion of the cable car "flying" over a massive boulder. Time your shot so the car appears to hover just above the rock for a surreal effect.

3. Misty Valleys in Autumn

If you visit in autumn, the valleys below are often filled with mist, creating a dreamy, layered effect. The mix of red and gold foliage peeking through the fog is pure magic.

The Yuping Cable Car: The Classic Route

The Yuping Cable Car is the oldest and most direct route to Huangshan’s central scenic area, making it a favorite for photographers aiming to capture the mountain’s most famous landmarks.

1. The Jade Screen Peak

As you near the top, the Jade Screen Peak comes into view—a towering formation often shrouded in mist. The interplay of light and shadow here is mesmerizing, especially during late afternoon.

2. The Celestial Capital Peak

From the cable car, you can glimpse the Celestial Capital Peak, one of Huangshan’s steepest summits. The jagged silhouette against the sky is a dramatic subject, especially at sunrise.

3. The "Monkey Gazing at the Sea" Rock

This iconic rock formation resembles a monkey sitting on a cliff, gazing into the distance. From the cable car, you can capture it from a unique angle, with the valley stretching out behind it.

The Xihai Cable Car: A Hidden Gem

The Xihai Cable Car is the newest and least crowded, offering access to the stunning Xihai Scenic Area. This route is perfect for those seeking solitude and unspoiled beauty.

1. The "Dreamlike" Peaks

The Xihai area is known for its surreal, dreamlike rock formations. From the cable car, you’ll see clusters of jagged peaks emerging from the clouds—ideal for moody, atmospheric shots.

2. The "Fairy Walking Bridge"

A narrow bridge spans a deep chasm, often enveloped in mist. From above, it looks like something out of a fantasy novel. Use a telephoto lens to compress the scene for maximum impact.

3. Sunset Over the Western Steps

If you time your descent right, the setting sun casts a warm glow over the Western Steps, turning the granite into a fiery orange. This is one of the best sunset spots on the mountain.

Photography Tips for Cable Car Shoots

  • Use a fast shutter speed to avoid motion blur.
  • Shoot through clean windows—some cable cars have scratched or foggy glass, so try to find a clear spot.
  • Pack light—you’ll be standing for most of the ride, so a compact mirrorless camera or a high-end smartphone is ideal.
  • Shoot in RAW to capture maximum detail in the shadows and highlights.

Huangshan’s cable cars aren’t just a means of transportation—they’re moving viewpoints offering some of the most spectacular photo opportunities in China. Whether you’re chasing the perfect sunrise shot or a misty valley scene, these routes deliver unforgettable moments at every turn.
