Huangshan Airport: Essential Information for First-Time Visitors

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Anhui Province, Huangshan Airport (TXN) serves as the primary gateway to one of China’s most breathtaking destinations—the iconic Huangshan Mountain. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a photography enthusiast, or simply seeking a serene escape, this airport is your first step toward an unforgettable adventure. Here’s everything you need to know to navigate Huangshan Airport like a pro.

Getting to Know Huangshan Airport

Location and Accessibility

Huangshan Airport is located approximately 5.5 kilometers (3.4 miles) from Tunxi District, the urban center of Huangshan City. The airport’s proximity to downtown makes it incredibly convenient for travelers. Taxis are readily available, with a ride to the city center taking about 15 minutes and costing around 30 RMB. Alternatively, public buses (Line 19) offer a budget-friendly option for just 2 RMB.

Flight Connections

While Huangshan Airport is relatively small compared to major international hubs, it offers a solid network of domestic flights. Direct routes connect to cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and Xi’an. International travelers typically connect through larger airports like Shanghai Pudong (PVG) or Beijing Capital (PEK).

Pro tip: If you’re flying internationally, allow ample layover time (at least 3-4 hours) for customs and baggage transfer.

Navigating the Airport

Terminal Layout

Huangshan Airport has a single terminal divided into two floors:
- Arrivals (Ground Floor): Baggage claim, customs (for international flights), and ground transportation options.
- Departures (Upper Floor): Check-in counters, security, and boarding gates.

Despite its compact size, the airport is well-organized, with clear signage in both Chinese and English. Free Wi-Fi is available, though you may need a local SIM card or VPN for seamless connectivity.

Check-In and Security

Domestic flights recommend arriving 1.5 hours before departure, while international travelers should aim for 2 hours. Security checks are efficient, but during peak travel seasons (April–November), lines can get longer.

Note: Liquids over 100ml are not permitted in carry-ons, and power banks must be under 20,000mAh.

Ground Transportation Options

Taxis and Ride-Hailing

Taxis are the most convenient way to reach your hotel or Huangshan Mountain’s base. Ensure the driver uses the meter or agree on a fare beforehand. Ride-hailing apps like Didi also operate in the area.

Airport Shuttles

Shared shuttles run between the airport and popular tourist areas, including Tangkou Village (the main entry point to Huangshan Mountain). Tickets cost around 20–30 RMB and can be booked at the arrivals hall.

Car Rentals

For those planning to explore beyond Huangshan, renting a car is an option. Major agencies like Hertz and Avis have counters at the airport. However, be prepared for narrow mountain roads and limited parking near scenic spots.

Nearby Attractions and Stops

Tunxi Old Street

Just 15 minutes from the airport, this historic pedestrian street is perfect for a quick cultural immersion. Sample local snacks like stinky tofu (chou doufu) or pick up handcrafted souvenirs like Hui-style inkstones.

Xidi and Hongcun Villages

These UNESCO-listed ancient villages, about an hour’s drive from the airport, offer a glimpse into traditional Anhui architecture. Ideal for a half-day trip if your flight arrives early.

Tips for a Smooth Experience

  • Language: While English is limited, translation apps like Google Translate (or Pleco for Mandarin) are lifesavers.
  • Currency: ATMs are available, but smaller vendors may prefer cash (RMB).
  • Weather: Huangshan’s climate is unpredictable—pack layers and rain gear, especially if heading to the mountain.

Final Thoughts

Huangshan Airport may be small, but it’s your portal to a world of misty peaks, ancient villages, and vibrant culture. With these tips in hand, you’re ready to start your journey with confidence. Safe travels—and don’t forget your camera!
