Where to Stay When Climbing Huangshan

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Huangshan, or the Yellow Mountains, is one of China’s most iconic destinations, renowned for its breathtaking granite peaks, ancient pine trees, and ever-changing sea of clouds. Whether you're an avid hiker, a photography enthusiast, or simply seeking a serene escape, choosing the right accommodation can make or break your trip. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, here’s everything you need to know about where to stay when climbing Huangshan.

Staying On the Mountain vs. At the Base

One of the first decisions you’ll need to make is whether to stay on the mountain or at its base. Each option has its pros and cons, depending on your travel style and priorities.

1. Staying On the Mountain

If you want to catch sunrise or sunset over the peaks—arguably the most magical moments on Huangshan—you’ll need to stay overnight on the mountain. Here are the best options:

Luxury Hotels

  • Xihai Hotel (Xīhǎi Bīnguǎn): Located near the famous Xihai Grand Canyon, this 4-star hotel offers stunning views and comfortable rooms. It’s a great choice for those who want easy access to sunrise spots like Danxia Feng.
  • Beihai Hotel (Běihǎi Bīnguǎn): Situated near the Beihai Scenic Area, this hotel is ideal for travelers who prioritize convenience. The nearby Lion Peak and Dawn Pavilion are perfect for sunrise photography.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Baiyun Hotel (Báiyún Bīnguǎn): A more affordable choice near the Bright Summit (Guāngmíng Dǐng), offering basic but clean accommodations.
  • Shilin Hotel (Shílín Bīnguǎn): Close to the iconic "Flying Over Rock," this hotel is popular among backpackers.

Dormitory-Style Lodging

For solo travelers or those on a tight budget, shared dormitories are available at several mountain lodges. While amenities are minimal, the experience of waking up above the clouds is unforgettable.

2. Staying At the Base

If you prefer modern comforts or plan to explore nearby attractions like Hongcun or Xidi villages, staying at the base of Huangshan might be a better fit.

Tunxi (Huangshan City)

  • InterContinental Huangshan Tunxi: A high-end option with spa facilities and easy access to Tunxi Old Street.
  • Huangshan International Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly pick with a lively atmosphere and helpful travel tips.

Tangkou Town

This is the closest town to the mountain’s main entrance, making it a convenient base for early-morning ascents.
- Huangshan Shilin Hotel: A mid-range hotel with shuttle services to the cable car station.
- Pine Valley Lodge: A cozy boutique hotel with a focus on sustainability.

Unique Accommodation Experiences

For travelers looking for something beyond standard hotels, Huangshan offers a few hidden gems:

1. Traditional Guesthouses in Hongcun

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hongcun is famous for its well-preserved Hui-style architecture. Staying in a centuries-old guesthouse adds a cultural layer to your trip.

2. Glamping Near Huangshan

For those who love nature but aren’t keen on roughing it, glamping sites around the mountain offer luxury tents with panoramic views.

3. Temple Stays

Some monasteries on Huangshan, like the Ciguang Temple, offer simple lodging for visitors seeking a spiritual retreat.

Booking Tips for Huangshan Accommodations

  • Book Early: Mountain hotels fill up quickly, especially during peak seasons (spring and autumn).
  • Check for Packages: Some hotels offer bundled deals with park tickets or guided tours.
  • Consider Weather: Higher-altitude lodges can get chilly, even in summer—pack accordingly.

Final Thoughts on Making the Most of Your Stay

Whether you choose a lavish mountain resort or a humble hostel, your Huangshan adventure will be shaped by where you rest your head. Plan wisely, and you’ll wake up to some of the most awe-inspiring views on Earth.
