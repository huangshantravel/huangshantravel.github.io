Huangshan Airport: The Best Way to Reach the Yellow Mountains

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Anhui Province, the Yellow Mountains (Huangshan) are one of China’s most iconic natural wonders. With their mist-shrouded peaks, ancient pine trees, and breathtaking sunrises, it’s no surprise that this UNESCO World Heritage Site draws millions of visitors each year. But before you can immerse yourself in this ethereal landscape, you’ll need to figure out the best way to get there. Enter Huangshan Tunxi International Airport (TXN), the gateway to the Yellow Mountains.

Why Fly into Huangshan Airport?

Convenience and Time-Saving

For travelers coming from major Chinese cities like Beijing, Shanghai, or Guangzhou, flying into Huangshan Airport is the fastest and most convenient option. The airport is just 12 kilometers (7.5 miles) from downtown Huangshan City and about 50 kilometers (31 miles) from the Yellow Mountains scenic area. Compared to long train or bus rides, a flight drastically cuts down travel time, leaving you with more energy to explore.

Well-Connected Domestic Routes

Huangshan Airport serves as a regional hub with direct flights from over 20 domestic cities, including:
- Beijing (2-hour flight)
- Shanghai (1.5-hour flight)
- Guangzhou (2-hour flight)
- Chengdu (2.5-hour flight)
- Xiamen (1.5-hour flight)

Seasonal international flights also connect Huangshan to popular destinations like Seoul and Taipei, making it accessible for global travelers.

Scenic Approach

Flying into Huangshan offers a unique aerial preview of the region’s dramatic landscapes. On clear days, passengers can catch glimpses of the jagged peaks and rolling clouds that define the Yellow Mountains—a teaser of the beauty awaiting them on the ground.

Getting from Huangshan Airport to the Yellow Mountains

Once you land, you’ll need to make your way to the mountains. Here are the best options:

Private Transfer or Taxi

The most hassle-free method is booking a private car or taxi. The ride takes about 1 to 1.5 hours, depending on traffic and your exact destination (e.g., Tangkou Town, the main entry point to the mountains). Many hotels and tour operators offer pre-arranged transfers for added convenience.

Airport Shuttle Bus

For budget-conscious travelers, the airport shuttle bus is a reliable choice. Buses run regularly to Huangshan City’s long-distance bus station, where you can catch another bus to the Yellow Mountains. Total travel time is around 2 hours.

Public Bus

Local buses (Line 19) connect the airport to downtown Huangshan, but this option requires transfers and is less straightforward for first-time visitors.

Tips for a Smooth Journey

Best Time to Visit

The Yellow Mountains are stunning year-round, but the most popular seasons are:
- Spring (April–June): Mild weather and blooming flora.
- Autumn (September–November): Crisp air and golden foliage.
- Winter (December–February): Snow-capped peaks and fewer crowds.

Summer brings lush greenery but also higher humidity and tourist numbers.

Packing Essentials

  • Layered clothing: Temperatures can vary drastically between the base and summit.
  • Sturdy hiking shoes: The trails are steep and often slippery.
  • Rain gear: Sudden showers are common in the mountains.
  • Camera: You’ll want to capture every breathtaking view.

Booking Flights in Advance

Huangshan Airport is small but busy, especially during peak seasons. Secure your flights early to avoid inflated prices or sold-out seats.

Exploring Beyond the Yellow Mountains

While the Yellow Mountains are the star attraction, the region has much more to offer:

Hongcun and Xidi Villages

These ancient UNESCO-listed villages are famous for their well-preserved Huizhou architecture, featuring whitewashed walls, black-tiled roofs, and intricate carvings. A 1.5-hour drive from the airport, they’re perfect for a cultural detour.

Tunxi Old Street

Located near the airport, this historic street is lined with traditional shops selling tea, inkstones, and local snacks like stinky tofu (chou doufu) and Huangshan sesame cakes.

Hot Springs

After a day of hiking, unwind at one of Huangshan’s natural hot springs, such as those in Tangkou Town. The mineral-rich waters are said to have healing properties.

Final Thoughts

Huangshan Airport isn’t just a transit point—it’s the starting line for an unforgettable adventure. Whether you’re a nature lover, a photographer, or a culture enthusiast, the Yellow Mountains and their surroundings promise a journey filled with awe and inspiration. So book that flight, pack your bags, and get ready to experience one of China’s most magical destinations.
