Huangshan to Hangzhou: Best Ways to Travel

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The journey from Huangshan to Hangzhou is one of China’s most scenic routes, blending natural wonders with cultural richness. Whether you're a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, this trip offers something unforgettable. Below, we break down the best ways to travel between these two iconic destinations, along with must-see stops and insider tips.

By High-Speed Train: Fast and Scenic

Why Take the Train?

The high-speed rail (HSR) is the most efficient way to travel from Huangshan to Hangzhou. The ride takes just under 2 hours, covering approximately 230 kilometers. Trains depart frequently from Huangshan North Station (黄山北站) and arrive at Hangzhou East Station (杭州东站).

What to Expect

  • Comfort: Spacious seats, clean cabins, and onboard amenities like Wi-Fi (on select trains).
  • Views: Large windows offer glimpses of rural Anhui and Zhejiang’s lush landscapes.
  • Cost: Tickets range from $20 to $40 USD, depending on class (Second Class, First Class, or Business Class).

Pro Tip

Book tickets in advance via platforms like Trip.com or Ctrip, especially during holidays like Golden Week or Spring Festival.

By Bus: Budget-Friendly but Longer

The Bus Experience

For travelers on a tight budget, long-distance buses are an option. The journey takes around 4-5 hours, with stops in smaller towns like Tunxi (屯溪) or Shexian (歙县).

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Cheaper (around $10-$15 USD), flexible schedules, and a chance to see off-the-beaten-path scenery.
  • Cons: Less comfortable, potential traffic delays, and limited legroom.

Insider Advice

Opt for sleeper buses if available—they’re more comfortable for overnight trips.

By Private Car or Taxi: Flexibility and Comfort

Renting a Car

Renting a car gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace. The drive takes about 3.5 hours via the G56 Hangrui Expressway.

Key Stops Along the Way

  1. Xidi and Hongcun Villages: UNESCO-listed ancient villages with stunning Hui-style architecture.
  2. Qiandao Lake (千岛湖): A breathtaking man-made lake with over 1,000 islands. Perfect for a detour.

Cost Considerations

  • Rental cars start at $50/day, excluding tolls and gas.
  • Hiring a private driver costs around $150-$200 for the one-way trip.

By Air: Quick but Less Practical

Flight Options

While there are no direct flights from Huangshan Tunxi International Airport (TXN) to Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport (HGH), connecting flights via Shanghai or Beijing are available.

Is It Worth It?

  • Pros: Saves time if you’re coming from afar.
  • Cons: Layovers can make the trip longer than the train, and tickets are pricier ($100-$300 USD).

Must-Visit Attractions in Hangzhou

Once you arrive in Hangzhou, these spots are unmissable:

West Lake (西湖)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, West Lake is the heart of Hangzhou. Rent a bike or take a boat ride to explore its pagodas, gardens, and tea plantations.

Lingyin Temple (灵隐寺)

One of China’s most famous Buddhist temples, surrounded by lush forests and intricate rock carvings.

Hefang Street (河坊街)

A lively pedestrian street packed with traditional snacks, souvenirs, and silk shops. Don’t miss trying dongpo pork (东坡肉) or longjing tea (龙井茶).

Seasonal Travel Tips

Best Time to Go

  • Spring (March-May): Cherry blossoms at West Lake and pleasant weather.
  • Autumn (September-November): Golden ginkgo trees and fewer crowds.

What to Avoid

  • Summer: High humidity and tourist crowds.
  • Winter: Chilly weather, though Huangshan’s snowscapes are magical.

Hidden Gems Near the Route

Wuyuan (婺源)

A detour to this "most beautiful village in China" is worth it for its油菜花 (rapeseed flower) fields in spring.

Longjing Tea Plantations

Just outside Hangzhou, these terraced fields offer tea-tasting sessions and stunning views.

Final Travel Hacks

  • Use Alipay or WeChat Pay: Cashless payments are king in both cities.
  • Download DiDi: China’s Uber equivalent for hassle-free rides.
  • Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases: While Hangzhou is tourist-friendly, a few words like xièxie (谢谢) go a long way.

No matter how you choose to travel, the Huangshan-Hangzhou route promises adventure, beauty, and a deep dive into China’s cultural heartland.
