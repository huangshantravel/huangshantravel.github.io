Best Dining Options at Huangshan Airport

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling through Huangshan Airport (TXN) and looking for a great meal before your flight? Whether you're arriving, departing, or just passing through, the airport offers a variety of dining options to satisfy your cravings. From local Anhui delicacies to international fast-food chains, here’s your ultimate guide to the best places to eat at Huangshan Airport.

Local Flavors: Authentic Anhui Cuisine

1. Huangshan Delicacies

If you’re visiting Huangshan for the first time, don’t miss the chance to try authentic Anhui dishes. The airport features a few restaurants specializing in local flavors, such as:

  • Steamed Mandarin Fish (臭鳜鱼 Chòu Guì Yú): A fermented fish dish with a unique aroma and rich taste.
  • Bamboo Shoots with Ham (火腿炖笋): A savory combination of tender bamboo shoots and cured ham.
  • Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐): A must-try for adventurous eaters.

These dishes are perfect for travelers who want a last taste of Huangshan before leaving.

2. Tea Houses

Huangshan is famous for its tea, particularly Huangshan Maofeng (黄山毛峰). Several small tea shops in the airport offer freshly brewed tea, allowing you to relax before your flight. Pair your tea with traditional snacks like tea-infused pastries or sweet osmanthus cakes.

Quick Bites: Fast Food and Cafés

1. International Chains

For those who prefer familiar flavors, Huangshan Airport has a few international fast-food options:

  • KFC: A reliable choice for fried chicken and burgers.
  • Starbucks: Grab a coffee and a pastry if you need a quick caffeine boost.

These chains are ideal for travelers in a hurry or those who prefer standardized meals.

2. Chinese Fast Food

If you want something quick but still local, try:

  • Lanzhou Lamian (兰州拉面): Hand-pulled noodles in a savory beef broth.
  • Yangzhou Fried Rice (扬州炒饭): A classic Chinese comfort dish.

These spots are usually less crowded than international chains and offer a taste of regional Chinese cuisine.

Upscale Dining: Sit-Down Restaurants

1. Airport Lounges with Dining

If you have access to airport lounges (via business class or priority passes), some offer complimentary meals and drinks. Expect:

  • Buffet-style spreads with both Chinese and Western options.
  • Premium teas and alcoholic beverages.

This is a great way to enjoy a quiet meal away from the crowds.

2. Full-Service Restaurants

A few sit-down restaurants in the airport provide a more refined dining experience. Look for:

  • Hot pot options (especially in colder months).
  • Dim sum selections for a light yet satisfying meal.

Tips for Dining at Huangshan Airport

  • Timing: Peak hours (mornings and evenings) can be busy, so plan accordingly.
  • Payment: Most places accept mobile payments (WeChat Pay/Alipay), but having some cash is a good backup.
  • Dietary Restrictions: If you have specific needs, international chains may be the safest bet.

Whether you’re craving local specialties or just need a quick coffee, Huangshan Airport has something for every traveler. Enjoy your meal and safe travels!
