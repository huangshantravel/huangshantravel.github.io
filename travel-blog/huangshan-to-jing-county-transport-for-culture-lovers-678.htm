Huangshan to Jing County: Transport for Culture Lovers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

For travelers seeking a blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, the journey from Huangshan to Jing County is a must-experience route in China’s Anhui Province. This scenic corridor connects two of the region’s most iconic destinations—Huangshan Mountain, famed for its misty peaks and ancient pine trees, and Jing County, a hidden gem celebrated for its traditional paper-making techniques and well-preserved Huizhou architecture. Whether you’re an art enthusiast, history buff, or simply a wanderer in search of authenticity, this guide will help you navigate the transport options while uncovering the cultural treasures along the way.

Why This Route Matters

The Cultural Heart of Anhui

Huangshan (Yellow Mountain) is often the star attraction, drawing millions for its otherworldly landscapes immortalized in classical Chinese paintings. But just a few hours away, Jing County offers a quieter, equally profound cultural experience. Known as the birthplace of Xuan paper—a high-quality rice paper used for calligraphy and painting for over 1,500 years—Jing County is where ancient craftsmanship meets rural charm. The journey between these two locations isn’t just about getting from point A to B; it’s a passage through time, tradition, and breathtaking scenery.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Appeal

While Huangshan’s popularity means crowds, Jing County remains relatively undiscovered by international tourists. This makes it perfect for culture lovers who want to dive deeper into Anhui’s heritage without the hustle. The transport options reflect this balance—modern convenience paired with opportunities for slow travel.

Transport Options: From Buses to Private Rides

By Public Bus

The most economical way to travel from Huangshan to Jing County is by public bus. Buses depart regularly from Huangshan Bus Station (near Tunxi Old Street) and take approximately 2.5 to 3 hours. Tickets cost around 40-50 RMB, and the ride offers glimpses of rural Anhui—terraced fields, quaint villages, and winding rivers.

Pros:
- Affordable and frequent departures.
- Authentic local experience.

Cons:
- Limited English signage; have your destination written in Chinese (Jīng Xiàn 泾县) to show the driver.
- Buses can be crowded during peak seasons.

By Private Car or Taxi

For those prioritizing comfort and flexibility, hiring a private car or taxi is ideal. The drive takes about 2 hours via the G3 Beijing-Taipei Expressway, with stunning mountain views along the way. Prices range from 300-500 RMB depending on negotiation and vehicle type.

Tips:
- Book through reputable apps like Didi or ask your hotel to arrange a driver.
- Consider making stops at lesser-known villages like Hongcun or Xidi en route.

By Train (Indirect Route)

While there’s no direct train to Jing County, you can take a high-speed rail from Huangshan North Station to Xuancheng (about 1 hour), then transfer to a local bus or taxi (another 1-hour ride). This option is less convenient but works if you’re combining your trip with other destinations.

Must-See Stops Along the Way

1. Xidi and Hongcun Villages

A slight detour from the main route, these UNESCO-listed villages are textbook examples of Huizhou architecture—whitewashed walls, black-tiled roofs, and intricate carvings. Hongcun’s moon-shaped ponds and Xidi’s ancient alleyways are straight out of a watercolor painting.

2. Taiping Lake

Halfway between Huangshan and Jing County, Taiping Lake is a serene spot for a break. Rent a boat, hike the lakeside trails, or simply enjoy the tranquility before continuing your journey.

3. Jing County’s Xuan Paper Workshops

Once in Jing County, head straight to the Xuan paper workshops. Watch artisans transform mulberry bark into delicate sheets of paper using techniques unchanged for centuries. Some workshops even let visitors try their hand at the craft.

Insider Tips for Culture Lovers

  • Timing: Visit Jing County during the Xuan Paper Cultural Festival (usually in autumn) for live demonstrations and exhibitions.
  • Food: Don’t miss local specialties like Jing County tofu and bamboo shoots, best enjoyed at family-run eateries.
  • Photography: The early morning fog in Jing County’s countryside creates a dreamy backdrop for photographers.

Final Thoughts

The journey from Huangshan to Jing County is more than a transfer—it’s a cultural pilgrimage. Whether you choose the slow charm of a bus ride or the ease of a private car, each mile reveals layers of Anhui’s artistic and historical legacy. Pack your curiosity, a good camera, and perhaps an empty notebook (to test that Xuan paper!), and let this route unfold its stories.
