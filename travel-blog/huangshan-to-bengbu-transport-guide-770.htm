Huangshan to Bengbu: Transport Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling from the breathtaking landscapes of Huangshan to the bustling city of Bengbu offers a unique blend of natural beauty and urban charm. Whether you're a backpacker, a luxury traveler, or someone simply exploring Anhui Province, this transport guide will help you navigate the journey seamlessly. Below, we’ll cover the best ways to travel between these two destinations, including trains, buses, and private transfers, along with tips to make your trip unforgettable.

Best Transport Options from Huangshan to Bengbu

1. High-Speed Train (G-Train)

The fastest and most convenient way to travel from Huangshan to Bengbu is by high-speed train (G-Train). The journey takes approximately 2 to 2.5 hours, making it ideal for travelers who want to save time.

  • Departure Stations: Huangshan North Station (黄山北站)
  • Arrival Station: Bengbu South Station (蚌埠南站)
  • Frequency: Multiple daily departures
  • Ticket Price: Around ¥100–¥150 (economy class)

Pro Tip: Book tickets in advance via 12306.cn or travel apps like Trip.com to secure the best seats.

2. Regular Train (K/T-Trains)

For budget-conscious travelers, regular trains (K or T-series) are a slower but cheaper alternative.

  • Travel Time: 4–5 hours
  • Ticket Price: ¥50–¥80 (hard seat)
  • Scenic Views: A great way to see the countryside at a relaxed pace

3. Long-Distance Bus

If you prefer road travel, long-distance buses operate between Huangshan and Bengbu.

  • Duration: ~5–6 hours
  • Price: ¥80–¥120
  • Comfort Level: Varies; some buses offer reclining seats

Note: Buses are less frequent than trains, so check schedules in advance.

4. Private Car or Taxi

For maximum flexibility, hiring a private car or taking a taxi is an option, though it’s the most expensive.

  • Travel Time: ~4 hours (depending on traffic)
  • Cost: ¥800–¥1,200
  • Best For: Groups or travelers with heavy luggage

Must-Visit Stops Along the Way

1. Hefei (合肥)

If you have extra time, consider stopping in Hefei, Anhui’s capital. Highlights include:

  • Sanhe Ancient Town (三河古镇) – A well-preserved water town
  • Hefei Swan Lake (合肥天鹅湖) – A scenic urban park

2. Chaohu Lake (巢湖)

One of China’s largest freshwater lakes, perfect for a quick detour.

  • Activities: Boating, cycling, and lakeside dining

Travel Tips for a Smooth Journey

1. Best Time to Travel

  • Spring & Autumn: Ideal for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Avoid Holidays: Chinese New Year and Golden Week can mean packed trains.

2. Packing Essentials

  • Comfortable Shoes: For exploring Huangshan’s trails.
  • Layers: Weather can change quickly in mountainous areas.

3. Local Eats to Try

  • Huangshan Stinky Tofu (黄山臭豆腐) – A bold-flavored snack.
  • Bengbu Roast Duck (蚌埠烤鸭) – A local specialty.

Exploring Bengbu: What to Do After Arrival

Once you reach Bengbu, dive into its vibrant culture:

1. Longzihu Park (龙子湖公园)

A serene lakeside park perfect for relaxation.

2. Bengbu Museum (蚌埠博物馆)

Learn about the city’s history and ancient artifacts.

3. Local Night Markets

Try street food like lamb skewers and jianbing (煎饼).

Final Thoughts on Transportation Choices

Each transport option offers different perks—whether it’s speed, cost, or scenery. Plan ahead based on your priorities, and enjoy the journey as much as the destination!
